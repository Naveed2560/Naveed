Why is Deforestation Happening?

In Aotearoa (New Zealand) and globally, deforestation is often linked to human-driven activities. Here's a quick breakdown:

  • Logging: Cutting down trees for timber and wood-related industries — sometimes done illegally or without replanting.
  • Farming Expansion: Native forests are replaced with large-scale agricultural fields, reducing biodiversity.
  • Urban Growth: Cities and roads continue to grow, pushing into green zones and disrupting ecosystems.
  • Consumer Demand: Everyday products like palm oil, packaging, and paper fuel forest loss in less visible ways.

Raising awareness is key — even small individual actions can help slow down deforestation. It's all about making conscious choices.
