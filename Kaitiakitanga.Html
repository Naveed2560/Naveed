Deforestation and Kaitiakitanga

Deforestation is the large-scale clearing of forests for farming, logging, or development. It destroys habitats, reduces biodiversity, and contributes to climate change.

Kaitiakitanga is a MƒÅori principle of guardianship that reminds us people are responsible for caring for the land, forests, rivers, and skies. Nature is a taonga (treasure) to be protected for future generations.

Practicing kaitiakitanga can mean planting trees, protecting native forests, and choosing sustainable products to reduce pressure on our environment.
