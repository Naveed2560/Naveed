What is Kaitiakitanga?

Kaitiakitanga is a Māori concept that means guardianship, protection, and care for the environment. It is deeply rooted in the culture and values of Aotearoa New Zealand. At its heart, Kaitiakitanga reminds us to look after Papatūānuku (Mother Earth) and everything that lives on her.

Practising Kaitiakitanga means:

  • Respecting the land, water, and forests as living ancestors that provide for us.
  • Using natural resources responsibly so they remain for future generations.
  • Working together with communities to restore and protect natural areas.

When we embrace Kaitiakitanga, we’re not just protecting nature—we’re creating a balanced relationship with the world around us. It’s about understanding that our actions today shape the environment of tomorrow. By caring for the land and each other, we ensure a healthy, sustainable future for all living things.
